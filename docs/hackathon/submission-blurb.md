# Encode submission — project description

Two lengths for the submission form. Rubric-aware ordering: UX first, the name-able idea second, the adoption story third, tech last. Both are honest — testnet stays stated.

## 150-word

> Rally is one link that lets any group — a trip chat, a family, a savings committee — move money together. Friends open it, sign in with email, and chip in USDC from whatever chain they hold it on. No wallet, no gas, no bridges — just a bar that fills, live. The pot enforces the group's condition: a **Goal** pays out when it's hit, or refunds every backer; a **Circle** rotates the pot each round — one missed round and everyone is refunded, automatically. That's the money PayPal abandoned (Money Pools, never replaced) plus the world's largest informal financial instrument (rotating savings circles), with the trusted middleman replaced by an ownerless contract. Underneath: Magic email login mints an embedded EIP-7702 wallet, ZeroDev sponsors every transaction, Circle CCTP v2 moves the USDC, and two verified, audited vaults on Arbitrum hold the pot. The refund rail is already proven onchain. Testnet only.
